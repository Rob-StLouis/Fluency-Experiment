University of California, San Diego Consent to Act as a Research Subject Study of Information Processing

Galit Yavne, M.A., Liam Kavanagh, M.A., Evan Carr, M.A., Andy Arnold, M.A. and Piotr Winkielman, Ph.D. request your participation in a study of information processing. You have been asked to participate because you indicated willingness to take part in an information processing study. There will be approximately 150 participants in the study. If you agree to be in this study, you will be shown words, pictures, short movies, pieces of music, and food items. You will be asked to make judgments about those stimuli. You may also be asked to indicate your feelings on a questionnaire. Risks involved in participating in this study include a feeling of frustration, fatigue or boredom.

Participation will take between two minutes to two hours (as detailed in the HIT), you will be compensated at the rate detailed in the HIT summary. Participation in this study is entirely voluntary and you may refuse to participate or withdraw at any time without penalty. The alternatives to participation in this study are to simply not participate. This research is supported by the NIMH (National Institute for Mental Health). You may call the UCSD Human Research Protection Program at (858) 657-5100 to ask about your right as a research subject or to report research related problems. You will not receive any direct benefit from your participation, but the knowledge gained by the investigators will advance the progress of psychological science and may help others in the future. For further information about this study contact: Piotr Winkielman, PhD, Department of Psychology, University of California, San Diego, 9500 Gilman Drive, La Jolla, CA 92093-0109, by phone at (858) 822-0682 or by e-mail at pwinkiel@ucsd.edu.
